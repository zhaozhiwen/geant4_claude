Cherenkov closure test: 1 GeV e⁻ on 1 m CO₂ at 1 atm

Geant4 v11.4.0 · plugin geant4-claude v0.0.4 · last updated 2026-05-14

Summary

A 1 GeV electron is fired along the +z axis through a 1 m × 1 m × 1 m block of CO₂ gas at 1 atm; an "ideal" downstream flux backplate records every optical photon that crosses it. Over 1000 events the simulation observed 161.35 ± 0.40 (SEM) Cherenkov photons per electron, against a Frank-Tamm prediction (integrated over the wavelength-dependent refractive index from the GDML RINDEX table, 200–775 nm) of 160.98 photons per electron — agreement at 0.93 σ. The radial distribution on the backplate is a flat plateau ending at the geometric endpoint R = (D + L/2) · tan θc = 3.15 cm, matching the simulation's rp90 to 1.3 %. All three closure checks PASS.

Setup

Geometry  (geometries/cherenkov.gdml)

  • World: 2 m × 2 m × 2 m cube, air_optical (N+O+Ar+C, ρ = 1.205 mg/cm³), RINDEX = 1.0 across 1.6–6.2 eV.
  • Radiator: 1 m × 1 m × 1 m cube of CO2_gas (1 C + 2 O, ρ = 1.842 mg/cm³ at 1 atm, 293 K), centered at the origin. RINDEX from the Bideau-Mehu Sellmeier fit (11-point table, 1.6–6.2 eV, n = 1.000414 → 1.000580).
  • Backplate: 2 m × 2 m × 1 mm slab of air_optical, centered at z = +51.05 cm (1 cm gap from CO₂ +z face), tagged sensitive via auxiliary auxtype="sensitive" auxvalue="cherenkov_backplate".

Results — closure test

Frank-Tamm prediction

Photon yield in the energy variable, with β = 1 and wavelength- dependent refractive index: 

N = (α / ℏc) · L · ∫E₁E₂ [1 − 1/(β² n²(E))] dE

α = 1/137.036    ℏc = 197.327 eV·nm    L = 1 m = 10⁹ nm
E ∈ [1.6 eV, 6.2 eV]  ⇔  λ ∈ [200 nm, 775 nm]
n(E) linearly interpolated from the GDML RINDEX table.

Geometric ring-radius prediction for a line emitter from z ∈ [−L/2, +L/2] hitting a screen at z = D, at fixed Cherenkov angle θc: 

r(z) = (D − z) · tan θc      ⇒      dN/dr = const on
[rmin, rmax] = [(D − L/2)tan θc, (D + L/2)tan θc]

D = 51.05 cm    L = 100 cm
θc = √(2(neff − 1)),  neff = yield-weighted mean n = 1.000486
        = 31.19 mrad
⇒ rmin = 0.033 cm,  rmax = 3.153 cm

Notes & interpretation

The closure is tight because every wavelength-dependent piece of the analytic prediction reuses the exact same RINDEX table the GDML hands to Geant4. The Frank-Tamm integral is evaluated in the energy variable with np.trapz on the 11-point grid; the simulation interpolates the same grid linearly in E at photon-generation time. Any mismatch from the Sellmeier curve cancels at the closure-test level — what remains is the actual physics-list accuracy of Geant4's Cherenkov implementation and the geometric assumptions of the analytic model.

Three things that surprised me, worth recording for future iterations:

  • The radial distribution is a flat plateau, not a ring. That's because the emitter is a 1 m line, not a point: the mapping zemit ↦ r is linear with constant Jacobian, so a uniform z gives a uniform r. The sharp endpoints of the plateau are a clean closure target: the simulation's rp90 matches geometry to 1.3 %.
  • The per-event yield is genuinely Poisson. Observed std 12.55 vs √μ = 12.69; the deviation is 1.1 %. Implication: the e⁻ track length variance through 1 m of CO₂ at 1 GeV is well below 1 %, consistent with the Bethe-Bloch / multiple-scattering regime at these energies.
  • Including the world and the backplate in the RINDEX table is load-bearing (per the saved memory). Omitting either kills photons silently between the radiator and the scorer. A same-day rerun without the world RINDEX would be a useful negative-control check.

Next experiments to consider: scan beam energy through the Cherenkov threshold (β · n = 1 ⇒ Ekin ≈ 5.4 MeV for e⁻ in CO₂ at 1 atm); swap CO₂ for C₄F₁₀ (n ≈ 1.00137) and verify yield scales linearly with (n − 1); scale to 10⁴ events to drop the radial closure to ~0.5 %.
